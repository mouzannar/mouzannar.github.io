Court Navigator

The justice system can feel distant, complex, and full of jargon. This guide is here to demystify it, not by showing you a chart, but by walking you through what court or tribunal you're likely to deal with and what that means for you.

How the system works

In England and Wales, different courts handle different types of legal problems. You’re not expected to know which one applies, we’ll guide you based on what your issue is about.

  • Tribunals handle things like benefits, immigration, or special educational needs and are often less formal than courts.
  • County Courts deal with most civil issues, like disputes over money, housing, or personal injury.
  • Family Courts deal with child arrangements, divorce, and protection from abuse.
  • Employment Tribunals handle problems like dismissal or discrimination at work.
  • Magistrates’ Courts handle most criminal cases and some family matters.
  • Higher courts (like the Crown Court, High Court, or Court of Appeal) handle more serious or complex cases or appeals.
